Coconut-Gochujang Glazed Chicken With Broccoli

Yield: 4 servings

Time: 20 minutes

Gochujang, a staple of Korean cooking, is a fermented red chile paste made from spicy Korean chile peppers and glutinous rice. Here, itâ€™s combined with coconut milk, which mellows the spicy gochujang into a creamy sauce. Fresh ginger is the key ingredient, infusing the sauce with brightness and spice that balance the rich coconut. This recipe calls for broccoli as the vegetable accompaniment, but cauliflower or brussels sprouts would also work. The sauce can be made in larger batches and used on grilled pork ribs, chicken wings or even pan-fried tofu.

Ingredients

  • 2 Tsp tablespoons canola oil
  • 1.5 lb Boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1 1/2-inch pieces
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 1 piece (2 inches) fresh ginger, peeled and cut into matchsticks (about 1/3 cup)
  • 0.5 cup cup unsweetened coconut milk
  • 3 Tbsp turbinado sugar or 2 tablespoons light brown sugar
  • 2 Tbsp gochujang paste
  • 2 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 lb broccoli florets, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • cooked rice, for serving
  • sliced scallions or chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish

Procedures

  1. Step 1

    Canola Oil
    Salt, Pepper, Ginger
    Chicken

    In a large nonstick skillet, heat oil over medium-high. Season chicken with salt and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden all over, about 3 minutes. Add ginger and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 2 minutes.

  2. Step 2

    Coconut milk
    Sugar, Gochujang, Soy Sauce

    Add coconut milk, sugar, gochujang and soy sauce and bring to a simmer, stirring until gochujang dissolves. Gently simmer over medium-low heat, stirring, until chicken is cooked through, about 5 minutes.

  3. Step 3

    Broccoli

    Meanwhile, in a saucepan of salted boiling water, blanch broccoli until crisp-tender, 2 minutes. Drain.

  4. Step 4

    Add button here

    Divide chicken and broccoli among plates; spoon with sauce. Serve with rice.
